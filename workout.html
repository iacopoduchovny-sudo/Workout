Program Notes

Your stats

164 lbs · 5'10" · ~14–15% body fat · Wide clavicles, wide iliac crest

Goal: mild cut to reveal abs (~11–12% BF), then build. Target loss: 0.5–0.75 lb/week.

Why U/L/U/L/U

Upper muscles get hit 3× per week — research (Schoenfeld et al.) shows higher frequency drives more hypertrophy. Lower days are separated by upper days so legs always recover. Rest days placed mid-week so you're never compounding fatigue.

The 2 sets to failure rule

Every set should end at 0–1 RIR (reps in reserve). This matches the hypertrophy stimulus of 4 moderate sets. If you hit the top of the rep range both sets → add weight next session. Progressive overload is #1.

Aesthetic frame strategy

  • Wide clavicles are your superpower — lean into them
  • Lat width visually narrows the waist from every angle
  • Side delts are the #1 shoulder width driver — 3 sets every upper day
  • No heavy weighted oblique work — keeps waist tight
  • Hip width is structural (iliac crest) — unfixable, but fixable optically
  • Practice stomach vacuums daily — tightens transverse abdominis

Rest / sleep

  • 7–9 hours — GH peaks during deep sleep
  • Active rest days still burn calories without blunting recovery
  • Swimming is ideal — full body, zero joint load, good for lats

Nutrition targets (cut phase)

  • Calories: ~2,050–2,200/day (250–400 deficit)
  • Protein: 165–180g/day (~1–1.1g per lb BW)
  • Timeline: 8–12 weeks to visible abs
  • Carbs around training, fats at other meals

Rep ranges decoded

  • Strength zone (6–10 reps): heavy compounds, mechanical tension
  • Hypertrophy zone (10–15 reps): sweet spot for growth
  • Pump zone (15–20 reps): metabolic stress, isolation finishers
